The most successful Web3 social media project that originated from or was significantly propelled by a hackathon is Lenster (now rebranded as Hey.xyz).

Success in this context is defined by its dominance as the primary web interface for the Lens Protocol, its consistent prize-winning history, and its active user base.

🏆 The Leading Success: Hey.xyz (formerly Lenster)
Lenster was the standout winner of the LFGrow Hackathon in 2022. It effectively became the "Twitter of Lens" and served as the proof-of-concept that showed the world what a decentralized social graph could look like in practice.

Prizes: It won multiple bounties during its initial hackathon phase and has since been a primary recipient of ecosystem grants from Aave/Lens.

Users: For years, it was the #1 web-based client for Lens Protocol. Even as the ecosystem has expanded, it remains the "gold standard" for desktop Web3 social interaction.

Why it succeeded: It offered a familiar, high-quality UX (similar to Twitter) but integrated Web3-native features like "collecting" posts as NFTs and profile ownership.

📱 The Mobile Success: Orb
While Lenster dominated the web, Orb emerged from the same hackathon ecosystem (specifically the LFGrow and subsequent Lens-focused events) to become the leading mobile-first Web3 social app.

Prizes: It won the "Best Mobile App" and several other categories across various Lens and Polygon hackathons.

Users: It has surpassed 100,000 users and is widely considered the most polished mobile social experience in Web3, featuring a "Professional" mode that mimics LinkedIn for the crypto community.

Unique Success: It was one of the first hackathon-born social apps to successfully implement NFT-gated communities, allowing creators to build private social spaces for their token holders.

🛠️ The Utility Success: Bountycaster
Within the Farcaster ecosystem, Bountycaster stands out as a project that started in the developer community (often featured in Farcaster-specific hackathons and "build weeks") and became a fundamental part of the network's economy.

Prizes: It has won numerous bounties from the Farcaster and Base (Coinbase L2) ecosystems.

Users/Utility: Unlike standard social apps, Bountycaster is a social marketplace. It allows users to post tasks for crypto rewards. It has facilitated thousands of transactions and is the primary way developers and creators get paid for small tasks within the Farcaster protocol.

📊 Comparison Summary
Project	Ecosystem	Origin	Primary Success Metric
Hey.xyz	Lens	LFGrow Hackathon	Most used Web Client; defined the UX for Lens.
Orb	Lens	Lens BUIDL-athons	100k+ users; #1 Mobile Social App on Lens.
Bountycaster	Farcaster	Farcaster Build Events	High "Economy-as-Social" utility; high retention.
Phaver	Lens/Cyber	Connect2023 Winner	170k+ MAU; arguably the largest user base.
[!NOTE]
Phaver is often cited as the biggest "winner," but it is important to note that while it won the Connect2023 hackathon for its CyberConnect integration, it was already a venture-backed project before that. In contrast, Hey.xyz and Orb are "pure" hackathon-to-startup success stories.
